Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if it's over five years old, shows cracks or damage, doesn't fit properly anymore, has worn-out straps or buckles, shows foam compression or styrene deterioration, or has been involved in a crash. Your helmet's integrity is vital for your safety, so don't hesitate to replace it if there are any concerns about its condition.

My bike has been in garage for too long. Is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that's been in storage, check tires for damage and inflation, test brakes, inspect drivetrain and frame for wear or damage, and ensure suspension (if applicable) is functioning. If uncertain, have a professional mechanic inspect it. Safety first!
